Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026

Readometer is a personal side project built by one person. This page covers what gets stored when you use it and what happens with that data.

1. Who runs this

Just me. For anything privacy-related, email chernonog.vova+readometer@gmail.com.

2. What we store

  • Your name and email address, from registration or Google Sign-In.
  • Books you add, reading sessions (pages read, time spent), and any goals you set.
  • Book metadata: titles, authors, ISBNs, cover images.
  • Basic server logs (IP address, user-agent) and a session cookie to keep you signed in.

3. Google Sign-In

If you sign in with Google, we get your name, email address, and profile picture. We don't access your Google Drive, Gmail, contacts, or anything else. Your use of Google Sign-In is also covered by Google's Privacy Policy.

4. How we use it

To run the app. We show you your reading data, keep your session going, and compute your reading stats. We don't sell your data or share it with anyone for advertising, or for anything else.

5. Cookies

One session cookie (readometer.*) keeps you signed in. No tracking, no analytics cookies, no third-party scripts.

6. How long we keep your data

Your data lives on our servers as long as your account exists. Delete your account and it's gone.

7. Your rights

You can ask to see, correct, or delete your personal data. Email chernonog.vova+readometer@gmail.com.

8. Changes

If this policy changes, the date at the top will reflect it. Continuing to use Readometer after an update means you're okay with the new version.
